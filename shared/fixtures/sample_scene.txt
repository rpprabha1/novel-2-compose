## Chapter 1

### Scene 1

Elena climbed the narrow attic staircase, one hand trailing along a bannister thick with dust. A single shaft of light fell through a high, cracked window, and motes drifted through it like slow snow. Somewhere below, the old house ticked and settled, the way old houses do.

At the top, she pushed open a door that hadn't been opened in years. The hinges shrieked. Inside, sheeted furniture stood in rows like sleeping animals, and a trunk sat alone under the window, its brass latches gone green with age.

She knelt and opened it. Her fingers found a stack of photographs, curling at the edges, and beneath them a letter, the ink browned to the color of tea. "If you are reading this," it began, "then I am already gone, and you have found the one room I asked them never to clean."

A floorboard creaked behind her. She turned, heart slamming, and found only the cat, orange and unbothered, picking its way across a shaft of dusty light toward the window ledge, where it sat and stared out at the darkening garden below.

Elena let out a breath she hadn't known she was holding, tucked the letter into her coat, and looked back once at the trunk before pulling the door shut behind her.
